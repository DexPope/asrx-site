Results Guide

Understanding Your Biomarkers

Got your results back? Here's what each marker is actually telling you — in plain language, no medical degree required.

Testosterone (Total)
Normal: 300–1000 ng/dL (men) · 15–70 ng/dL (women)

The primary anabolic hormone — drives energy, muscle mass, libido, mood, and metabolic rate.

⬇ Low — fatigue, low drive, brain fog, muscle loss, poor recovery
⬆ High — may indicate supplementation or adrenal issues
Related: Performance · Sexual Health
Cortisol
Normal (AM): 6–23 mcg/dL

Your primary stress hormone — regulates inflammation, blood sugar, immune response, and sleep cycles.

⬇ Low — chronic fatigue, immune dysfunction, poor stress tolerance
⬆ High — poor sleep, weight gain (belly fat), anxiety, immune suppression
Related: Cognitive · Anti-Aging
TSH (Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone)
Normal: 0.4–4.0 mIU/L

Controls thyroid hormone output. High TSH = underperforming thyroid; low TSH = overactive thyroid.

⬇ Low — hyperthyroid: rapid heartbeat, weight loss, anxiety, insomnia
⬆ High — hypothyroid: fatigue, weight gain, cold sensitivity, hair loss
Related: Anti-Aging · Weight Loss
IGF-1
Normal: 100–300 ng/mL (age-dependent)

Reflects growth hormone activity — critical for muscle growth, fat metabolism, cellular repair, and aging.

⬇ Low — poor recovery, muscle loss, reduced fat burning, accelerated aging
⬆ High — monitored under provider care; may indicate GH excess
Related: Performance · Anti-Aging
Fasting Glucose
Normal: 70–99 mg/dL · Pre-diabetic: 100–125

Blood sugar after fasting — key marker for metabolic health, insulin sensitivity, and diabetes risk.

⬇ Low — hypoglycemia: shakiness, confusion, fatigue between meals
⬆ High — insulin resistance, pre-diabetes, cardiovascular risk
Related: Weight Loss · Anti-Aging
Vitamin D (25-OH)
Optimal: 40–80 ng/mL · Deficient: <20

A hormone-like vitamin essential for immune function, bone density, mood, and testosterone production.

⬇ Low — immune dysfunction, low mood, bone loss, poor testosterone levels
⬆ High (>100) — rare; usually from excess supplementation
Related: Immune · Anti-Aging
Estradiol (E2)
Men: 10–40 pg/mL · Women: 30–400 pg/mL

Primary estrogen — affects mood, bone health, cardiovascular function, libido, and cognitive clarity.

⬇ Low — joint pain, low libido, brain fog, mood swings, bone loss
⬆ High (men) — water retention, gynecomastia, low testosterone symptoms
Related: Sexual Health · Anti-Aging
CRP (C-Reactive Protein)
Optimal: <1.0 mg/L · Elevated: >3.0

A key marker of systemic inflammation — elevated CRP is linked to cardiovascular disease and accelerated aging.

⬇ Low — healthy inflammatory baseline; ideal
⬆ High — chronic inflammation: poor recovery, cardiovascular risk
Related: Recovery · Immune
DHEA-S
Men: 100–500 mcg/dL · Women: 35–430 mcg/dL

Adrenal precursor hormone — supports energy, immune function, and is a key anti-aging biomarker.

⬇ Low — fatigue, poor immune response, low mood, accelerated aging
⬆ High — may indicate adrenal overactivity; acne, hair changes in women
Related: Anti-Aging · Performance
